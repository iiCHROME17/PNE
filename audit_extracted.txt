Priority actions before submission
1.  Reduce Chapter 5 by 800–1,000 words  [CRITICAL]
Chapter 5 — Design documentation overlap
Sections 5.1.1–5.1.3 repeat architectural information already covered in Chapter 4. The 'core vs supporting components' breakdown (5.1.2) can be condensed to a table. The NPC data structure prose (5.1.4) is very granular — move to an appendix or cut. Target: reduce from ~3,729 to ~2,800 words.
2.  Trim Chapter 3 literature review by 500–700 words  [HIGH]
Chapter 3 — Description before critique
Subsections 3.3.2 (Guimond) and 3.4.2 (Wooldridge) describe the source material at length before critiquing it. Compress description and foreground the critique. The 'gap the PNE fills' argument in 3.7 can be tightened by pulling in some of the sharper framing from Section 8.2. Target: ~4,800 words.
3.  Trim Chapter 7 discussion by 300–400 words  [MEDIUM]
Chapter 7 — Some passages re-summarise prior chapters
Several paragraphs in 7.1 restate results from Chapter 6 before analysing them. Tighten by removing restatement and leading directly with the analytical point. The limitation acknowledgements in 7.3 are slightly repetitive across sub-sections.
4.  Strengthen in-text citations in Chapters 4–6  [MEDIUM]
Chapters 4–6 — Sparse inline citation
The technical chapters have very few bracketed citations. Where formulas or design decisions are grounded in the literature (Ellis 1962 for emotional valence rules, Bratman 1987 for BDI, Heider 1958 for attribution logic), these should be cited inline at the point of use, not only in the literature review chapter.
5.  Fix appendix accessibility  [MEDIUM]
Appendices — External file references not visible in PDF
Appendices H, I, J, K, and L reference external Markdown files ('see Dissertation/Literature Material/Drafts/Graphs/...'). These will not be visible to a marker reading the PDF. Either inline the key tables into the appendix body, or add a clear note that they are in the submitted code repository with a path the marker can follow.
6.  Add limitations paragraph to Chapter 6  [LOW]
Chapter 6 — Evaluation limitations deferred entirely to Chapter 7
The results chapter should briefly acknowledge the log-only evaluation approach within the chapter itself. One short paragraph noting that results are drawn from system-generated logs and no human participant evaluation was conducted suffices — this pre-empts a marker's concern at the point of reading rather than making them wait for Chapter 7.
Strengths to preserve during editing
The following sections are high quality and should not be substantially altered during the downsizing pass:
Section 8.4 (Reflection) — personal, specific, and intellectually honest. Do not cut.
Section 8.2 (Originality of Contributions) — the comparison against Facade, Generative Agents, and Bordini et al. is precise and well-grounded.
Section 2.5 (Risk Register) — the table format with likelihood/impact/mitigation columns is exactly what the mark scheme rewards.
Chapter 2 overall — the three-tier studio analysis (small/mid/large) is one of the most sophisticated social impact discussions in any CS3IP dissertation.
The CHANGELOG narrative in Section 8.1 — tracing architectural decisions from observed failures is a genuine methodological contribution that differentiates this project.
Section 7.1 framing — separating deterministic layer validation from LLM output validation is intellectually mature and will impress an experienced marker.
Overall assessment
This is a high-quality, intellectually serious dissertation with a genuinely original system at its centre. The writing is confident and precise, the architecture is well-motivated and clearly explained, and the reflection is one of the strongest in the cohort.
The primary risks to the final mark are structural:
The word count overage directly threatens criterion #9 (Organisation & Presentation) — this is the most urgent fix.
The absence of any external evaluation (user study, expert review, or heuristic evaluation) caps criteria #4 and #6. This is a known limitation; it needs to be acknowledged proactively at the point of the results, not only in the discussion.
After trimming to within the 20,000-word limit and adding inline citations in the technical chapters, this dissertation is comfortably in the first-class 70–79% band, with a realistic path to 80%+ if the methodology limitation is addressed more proactively in Chapters 4 and 6.
Bottom line for Claude Code: Reduce body word count to under 20,000 by cutting Chapters 5 and 3 first. Do not touch the Reflection (8.4) or the Originality section (8.2). Add inline citations at formula/design-decision points in Chapters 4 and 5. Inline or annotate the external appendix file references.
