# Web of Sin

## Aurelia

I almost died from dreaming.

It  just felt so incredibly real.

It wasn’t lucid. It was apathetic. I was just there. Sitting alone in this alleyway with barbed fences on each side. I couldn’t leave. I just sat there, against the rain, looking around hopelessly. My eyes followed the big industrial pipes coming off the biggest building. Dark gunmetal colour. The rain clanked against them again and again. Some dark ominous melody chiming out. A haunted choir.

I was isolated, trapped, stuck. I had nothing to do, nowhere to look. My mind just began searching for detail in anything.

Even the gravel on the floor. Grey at first. Then you look longer and it changes. From a monotonous grey to an assortment of equally mundane colours. The ground begins to look like a network of veins. Small stones nested against each other, packed tight. Watching me. Waiting.

There was some hidden evil, surely.

I tried to scare myself into feeling something. That’s the honest truth. I thought if I could explain to myself this must be death, I could find peace in it. People die and go to hell. People die and rebirth into someone else. I had been sent to a desolate alleyway. This must be limbo.

That’s when it happened.

An Old Man opened a door. He came outside. I tried to look past him before the door closed. Just black. Nothing. Not a light, not a single visible thing in that building. He had no biotech installed. None. He looked like someone from those “wayback-when” pictures. Like something your great great grandmother shows you on a Sunday morning. She’s your grandma though, and you love her.

So you act like you’re fascinated.

He stood with his wrinkled face and a rotting mouth. He hardly ever opened it and I’m grateful to God that he didn’t. You could see the foul mass hiding in there. The words I’m expressing to you could never tell you how disgusting it was. My stomach turned just looking at the shape of his lips, the way they didn’t quite close over whatever was festering inside.

His eyes were wet. Streams of water flowing down his face but he wasn’t crying. It was like stagnant water could flow and the source were these pale eyes. No colour. Just this grey stonewash look. It ‘s like he’s blind but he’s staring into my soul.

I feel it.

His hands rest half up in the air. The world’s most unenthused zombie pose. His hands tremble, shake so rigorously you realise it’s not age. There’s something in him being exorcised and he’s trembling. You can see the grooves of his bones practically. His skin is more wrapper than flesh. All of him is just so fragile, so weak.

And all he did for the whole time was just stare at me.

Eventually you start thinking. Is this me? Is this what I’ll turn to? The environment was oppressive in a way that made time meaningless. Every atom around me felt too familiar, catalogued in my brain. The rain against the pipes. The gravel veins on the ground, the dark clouds pressing down. What was the worst was The Old Man’s wet eyes, fixed on mine.

I stood in the rain, alone in the presence of one of the most uncanny human beings I’d ever seen. I’ve met people who are ultramodded. Their skin even is biotech. I’ve met people who sacrificed their arms and legs literally, to be hooked by their neural interfaces to ultracomputers. An old, naturalistic hag is what scared my soul straight. I still see him sometimes when I close my eyes. Those grey eyes.

Staring.

I’ve gone out shopping to clear my mind. At first I tried to go on campus. A part of me thought a lecture would help, a professor talking endlessly about whatever for the day would get my mind off of it. I was wrong. I sat there surrounded by so many people and yet I still felt this weight, this loneliness. The dream hardwired this solitude into me. Every single time I blink it’s like I’m transported back there. I see him again. There’s no way I’d be able to pay attention to my classes. I need a drink, something strong like a whiskey or a spirit. I’m praying the alcohol would kick whatever’s in me out.

But it’s only eleven AM.

I’m in the G-Plaza. The biggest shopping centre in this city. The city’s too damn big. Malignant almost, it just keeps on growing and growing. They don’t add soul to the place, they don’t add culture. Instead you get commerce. Big neon signs, cramped residential towers, terrible ground traffic and even worse air traffic. The G-Plaza’s an almost-safe haven. The big white dome and the greenery makes it feel... stagnant and sterile. It’s the same as his eyes. Every single thing about the plaza is smooth. It’s gleaming under the weak mid-morning sun. Its curves were impossibly perfect, the dome a flawless capsule of glass and white polymer, reflecting light in an unnatural, clinical way. Inside, the floors arched in gentle, uninterrupted sweeps, guiding pedestrians along paths that felt predetermined, almost rehearsed. There were no corners, no rough edges.

Not a single imperfection.

Endlessly wandering up and down the centre I look window to window, sign to sign. Trying to find anything that could cheer me up. Nothing’s working. Every bit of tech reminds me of him. That’s all this place sells. Every single door leads to some kind of tech vendor. Limb rentals, Mood affectors, biocosmetics. The faint antiseptic tang of biocosmetics leaks into the air, clinical and wrong. A display of mood-affectors. All of it is just more tech, more slop for the rich to toy with, the drugs of the future, they call it. Seeing that Old Man just made the contrast clear as night and day. We’re never really sober.

I pass by a chrome pillar and catch my reflection. For a second there’s a green tint bleeding into the edges. Even looking at my own reflection makes me feel as fake as the rest of the world. I look away quickly, try to pay it no mind.

I just can’t ignore it anymore. The sterility. I see everything too clearly. We all live in this fog and you think you want the fog lifted. You really don’t. What the fog hides is human horror.

Reality.

I finally stumbled up to this one shop. It’s human. It’s real. The tension in my mind eases momentarily. They’re not selling technology. They are not selling subscriptions. Something real. It’s an old-fashioned jewellery place. The ones they had a very long time ago, those physical locations. Necklaces behind glass, rings and earrings too. Fancy stuff all before your eyes.

I stare at this one necklace. It’s beautiful. Some kind of pearl necklace, but the pearls are some other stone. My guess is some kind of Bismuth or something. Each pearl has this unique colour, they all reflect light differently, they all shine in their own gorgeous way. A seamless blend of golds, reds, pinks greens and blues. It looks like it shouldn’t work, but it just feels like it does. The necklace has this air of imperfection. Not a manufactured kind but some natural kind. It’s rejuvenating. It’s got soul.

I need it.

It took me minutes to realise I hadn’t thought of my dream. Even then, it became second class to the necklace. I looked just below it, down to the price prompter. Seventy-two thousand credits. Seventy-two. That’d take me better half of a year to be able to afford. And only if my other expenses miraculously disappeared. There’s no way I could afford it but something tells me I need to leave with it. I have to. I’m haunted and I see something that gives me the slightest symbol of peace. I can’t go back to being haunted.

The dream seriously makes me feel like I woke up with cyberschizoid disorder or something.

I’ve got to shake it off. I ask the lady to look at it. She thinks I’m suspicious. I’ve got to be acting weird in some way, I just can’t trust myself to behave normal in this state. I’m trying my best, but I know I’m not doing a good job. It’s a jewellery store too, she’s probably got some eye augmentations. Threat detectors. Some kind of silent security.

She hands me the necklace anyways.

The second it touches my palms, this wave of ultimate relief comes over me. I’m freed, finally I’m freed. I found something real, this necklace here is more of a friend to me than anyone else. It’s real, it’s tangible, it’s imperfect. Even the grooves in each pearl don’t feel the same, some feel rough, some feel smooth. What’s weirdest is they all feel cohesive. It’s the opposite of the gravel I mentioned to you.

There’s unity.

I hold it for a moment longer. The lady’s watching me. Time stretches. My thumb runs over the surface of one pearl, then another. Each one different. Each one proof that something can exist without being designed to death. My chest tightens.The thought appears before I can stop it: I could just walk out. Right now. Just leave. The exit isn’t far. She’s alone behind the counter.

I look up at her. She’s already suspicious. Her eyes narrow slightly. I should put it back. I should thank her and leave. I clear my voice, ready to say a simple thank you before handing it back.

But my legs just took off instead.

I started running, the lady didn’t even react as hard as I did. I was more astonished than she was. Why did I do it? My legs just exerted all the force they had and sped off. All I knew to do was to just run. I sped past all these faces, just staring blankly. Not a single one of them could care less. I stuck out like a sore thumb, running down these twists and turns trying to get away and not a single person would make a noise.

I saw the exit doors. I was homebound. A couple dozen more steps and I’d be a free woman with a free necklace. The light of the midday sky gleamed through the doors. It looked like heaven I was running into. Just before I stepped outside, the glass offered one last reflection. It wasn’t my face. It was his. I shook it off; the only thing that mattered now was that I finally, finally felt free.

Until I got outside.

Something tackled my legs instantly. I fell to the floor and slammed my face in the concrete. The world went dark for just a second or two. My entire face burned, my head rang. It felt like my brain was vibrating. I rolled over in agony, I’ll admit I didn’t even think about the fact I was caught, I just wanted to know where the necklace flew off to. I looked to my left, I looked to my right, all of it was pointless. My vision modules were recalibrating so everything became this blur. The only thing I could certainly see was a pair of humanoid robots. They walked up to me, in pure silence. They didn’t say a single word. It was inevitable, whatever I was thinking when I took that necklace, was ultimately stupid. Of course there was going to be security. Of course I’d get caught.

I’m an idiot.

One of them grabbed me and pulled me up to my feet. The world was still spinning. I could barely stand up. I buckled under the pressure of my dizziness and it immediately grabbed me and held me still. The other stood eye-level with me. It had sensors attached to its head. They were these strong blue lights. As it leaned in, they turned this crimson red, rays shot out from it. They were scanning me, identifying me. It really was over. From head to toe, toe to head, the robot analysed me. When it finished, it paused. I didn’t even feel suspense, I felt hopeless. Whatever outcome it chose would be grim.

“ Aurelia Waterson”

“WebOS has recorded the violation. The stolen Asset is logged. A four-hundred-and-eighty hour penalty has been uploaded to the WebFrame. The Mainframe will execute Order A4M2.”

Shit.

## Victor

I was sitting by my desk. Swirling around on my chair. Work this month had been awfully slow, it was so boring. I guess I shouldn’t complain. I’m a homicide detective. A slow month means nobody’s getting murdered. Still, it was kind of weird to me. This city is massive and there’s gangs everywhere. Some of them in the streets. Some of them hiding behind vendors. Some of them illicitly owned by the corporations. There’s always warfare somewhere and on some level.

Just last month there were four-hundred-and-twenty-two murders.

I get that it sounds crazy, the standard citizen probably would think it’s terrible. The thing is, when you get into more bureaucratic roles you realise how hopeless it all is. Even I want to move out as soon as possible. Hypercity life isn’t as promising as it was two decades ago. The longer you live here, the scarier it feels; atrocities are too common. Some of the cases I’ve had to solve, if I told you what happened you’d throw up on the spot.

So why do I do it?

I can’t even lie to anyone. It was a young me, looking for money. I didn’t have shit growing up. My education was terrible and I hated the village lifestyle. They still existed back then, there were probably like four-five in the whole country back then and I was cursed enough to be born in one. I hated it so much. Biotech was sparse, opportunities were low.

I knew I’d have to get out.

So I did. This place offered it all. The dream. If I became a Civil Operator, I’d have it all. So I did. I spent eight years with the SWAT, lost my leg and spent four years off-duty, until they gave me a new leg, advanced Biotech. Then, they offered me a senior position with Precinct 0AV, the biggest one in the city. I took it, sure, the money was Medusa. After time the role just took too much from me, I resigned my position and became a senior detective.

And that’s where we are today.

The phone rings by me. I instantly fix myself up, sit properly and clear my throat. I pick it up and announce myself.

“Hello, this is Victor Brownsley with P-0AV.”

“Victor!”

The voice instantly gave it away. It was my partner.

“Fabien. What’s wrong?” He sounded disturbed and urgent. I felt a wave of excitement met with a wave of guilt for feeling the first wave.

“There’s been a small scale terrorist attack! In District 6PX. Two beat cops. Five civilians. Six gang members.”

The way he said it made it feel a lot more than small scale. His tone, again it sounded so disturbed. You don’t know Fabien, he’s ex-paramilitary. He’s seen genocides.

What’s thirteen bodies to hundreds and thousands? The answer scares me.

“Get over here. Now. It’s 6PX Harold’s Lane, the Gran Aceria Restaurant.”

With that I immediately got up. I pulled open my desk drawers, scrambling to get everything I needed. My ID Card, my Pistol. All of it. I took one last glance at the photo on my desk. Strange, I usually forget it’s there. I walked over to the elevator and called it. Fabien gave me nothing. No details, no nothing, just the basics. He said six gang members. I started to theorise who it could be. 6PX. That’s Boscetti Area. Who could’ve been stupid enough to attack them?

I started making guesses on the way there.

When I arrived the scene was already blocked off. Hologram warnings and civilian restrictors everywhere. The scene was abnormally large. There was a car that crashed straight into a pole; it had come straight down on the car. The chassis completely caved in; the bonnet squashed. The car looked like a compact cube more than a car. Smoke leaked from every crevice it could. I switched my vision filter to low-vis and walked carefully to the car. Everything was a mess. I had to watch my step. Debris was everywhere. Glass from the Aceria, blood from who knows what, shrapnel, you name it. I stood peering into the car. There were four dead men. Their bodies were horribly dismorphed. It was a disgusting sight. The driver, he was the worst - assuming it was a he. Their head had been smashed into a bloody pulp by the pole. His body was crushed by the compaction.

“It’s horrible, isn’t it.” It was Fabien. He had quietly strolled beside me and made an observation.

I didn’t even say a word. I just nodded very gently. I’d been death-sober for almost a month, no bodies, no crime scenes. Just E-docs and case reviews. This? A grotesque reminder what humans are capable of. I looked into the back seats. Something caught my eye about the guy on the right side. He had on what looked like some kind of Pirated Biotech. Some underground modded scrapwear.

“These guys might be Pakrunners.”

Terrible lot. They make their dough through kidnapping and graverobbing. Taking tech from anyone they can. It makes them sound primitive, sure, but they’re scientists on some crazy cyberpsychedelics. They’re far from home. They stay around Northolt, 385PBK. That’s four-and-a-half hours northwest from here.

“Come here. Take a look at this.”

I carry myself to Fabien’s position. He’s looking down at these two dead bodies. The other gang members. A couple of standard cops surround him, some crime scene photographers too. They’re giving these guys special treatment compared to the meat sandwiches in the car. I stand over the bodies and -

“Holy shit.”

The words just escape from my mind to reality. The two dead bodies. They’re littered with holes, a real sponge full of bullets. Blood’s practically still gushing out of them. They’re in this massive pool of their own blood. The difference that creates the shock is you know exactly who they are. Niko and Tomasso Munscara; two of the biggest enforcers in the Munscara Family. The police databases even have nicknames for them, “The Two Dark Princes.” They’re more than street legends.

You learn about these guys.

I remember, even in my high school, criminology class 3. They were talked about. And this was some decades ago, back when they were just coming up they were still that known. They only grew too. I used to obsess about them when I was running the show in the precinct. They were responsible for at least twenty-five percent of the crimes we had to deal with. Now? They’re dead in a district they don’t own. The Munscara and the Boscetti have no gang politics. No reason to be out here, especially alone.

These guys have bounties. Big bounties. Niko, alone, there’s eighty-five thousand credits on his head.

“What the hell is this?” I had to ask. I really couldn’t piece anything together. It was too perplexing. I hadn’t even looked at the rest of the scene, and I didn’t want to either. It’d probably make me more confused.

“I know. The Dark Princes? Here? There’s something larger here.”

He was right. There was something beyond the scale of our understanding. As much as I hate to admit it, beyond our capabilities too. I’d wanted a case, sure. A case like this? We definitely do not have the resources.

“This works for BIGA, Fabe, Not us.” I had to say it. It makes no sense, sure, but I just got this hunch in me, that whatever was going on here, BIGA would solve it. They have everything we don’t have. Resources, personnel and the ability to intimidate gangs, enforce a RICO or something.

“Oh Vic, trust me, I have no desire to be responsible for this.” Fabien scoffed, “Just collect evidence and hope McGruffrey and them boys take the investigation over.”

I walk away from the bodies, taking Fabien’s instructions. I felt queasy. Two of the biggest criminals are dead, randomly. It’d be fair enough, if this was some two-bit gangsters. These are globally recognised criminals. They’re tied to the culture of this city. There’s no way some gang hit a driveby on them, it’s foreign to the both of them. That’s bullshit. Nobody could kill these two, just like that. Not even God himself. There’s at least 200 families that have been praying to God that he smites them.

I hear a groan.

It’s coming from the alleyway. Did someone survive? Is someone hurt? I instantly rushed to it. There’s drops of blood leading to a bin. Someone had been hit. They needed help quickly. I rushed over, pulling out some first aid kit out of my pocket. I couldn’t see them yet, they were hiding behind the dumpster. I got closer and closer, moving in a rush and the groans started to sound more and more...

Feminine.

Maybe it was a female gangster, maybe it was a civilian. I haul my ass over there, going from a brisk walk to a jog. It felt more urgent. I get to the dumpster and walk past it. Once there I look down.

And Oh my God.

The world started to darken. This insurmountable wave of fear crawled over me. It was horrifying. I couldn’t even speak, I couldn’t find the words to speak. My heart was pounding out of my chest. I literally fell to my knees. Right to her eye levels. Even I could tell the look of terror on my face must have been mortifying.

It was Nina. My Wife.

“Why? “ That’s the only thing I could say. There were so many questions in my mind. So, so many. I wanted to ask if she was okay. She clearly wasn’t. The words are lost describing where I am in my mind now. I’ve never once in my life felt such a strong sense of hopelessness before. It was like my entire world had fallen apart, turned to a liquid and stained on my cheek. The only thing stopping me from throwing up was that I didn’t want to waste a second. I had to save her.

“OS. Th- The Web. Baby. The Web. It - “ She was struggling to speak. The shock I was in didn’t make me think to tell her to be quiet. All I could do was scramble to find the right aid. My hands shaking, my sight blurring. My heads-up display gives me vital information on both me and her.

Neither of us were good.

“It’s him. Vic... It - It’s Fabien. He...” She stopped. Her eyes started to flutter. My eyes widened. Fabien? He what? The only words I made out of her mouth were Web and Fabien. My head started to feel heavier. I was fighting off the urge to faint too, on top of every other urge.

“Oh… That’s unprofessional of me. I left one.” I was paralysed. The coldness that came out that voice told me the perpetrator behind, this was behind me. The voice was too familiar. You probably know who it was too.

Fabien.

I’m on my knees now, while he’s pointing a gun at me. An execution. It was supposed to be my wife’s, but it’s ours now. I wanted to look at her one last time - I couldn’t, call me a coward but I refuse for my last image of her to be engrossed in blood and tears. I closed my eyes. For once, I felt myself praying to God. A desperate plea. I couldn’t care less for my own redemption. I know where I’m going, I know what I’ve done. I accept it. Nina, she’s not like me. The embodiment of virtue. She’s my saint. There is no way in hell justice can exist if she dies like this. With my eyes closed I make my final prayers that may justice in truth be served.

“Fabien, what have you done? Why? After all this?”

“This city’s a heartless place, brother. You gotta get it how you live it Victor.”

“ I don’t get it. I’m not going to beg you Fabien. Just tell me why. Why her.”

“I’ve got debts to pay, brother. The Gesetzlose has cash. It’s just business.”

The Gesetzlose? He did all of this for the German Mafia? I feel a pit of contempt form in my stomach. I’ve spent the better part of a decade with this man. I’ve been through thick and thin with him. I’ve killed people to save him. He’s done the same. He’s gotten shot saving me before. So it goes out like this? I still don’t have any of my other answers. The Gesetzlose being behind this doesn’t help me at all.

He cocks the gun.

I’ve got no time left. This is it. My ending. I knew I’d get no quiet death. It just didn’t make any sense knowing my line of work. This was really something else. My wife. What about my daughters? My youngest’s off to university in four months. My eldest is getting married in fifteen. My son’s preparing for the biggest basketball game in the country. We can’t go out like this. Who’s going to see them through? Who’s going to drop Anna off at University? Who’s going to walk Brielle down the aisle? Who’s going to applaud Ace?

I think about the picture on the desk. All of us, together - I contrast the picture with my wife’s unconscious body now. I’ve never, ever felt such a strong wave of hatred in my heart. The disgust eats at me. None of this is right.

“This is it, brother.”

“I’m not your brother.” That’s the last thing I could speak out into the world. When I was young I thought I’d go out in a blaze of glory. Say something stupid like “tell Nina I love her.” I couldn’t even afford that, now. All that was left was to vent out the hate in my heart.

Then it came.

Black.

My head shoots up. I look around. I’m alive, sat at my desk, like nothing ever happened. My hand flies straight up to my head. I feel my temple and my forehead in disbelief. There’s no blood, no bullets. I’m okay. What the fuck was that, a dream? It couldn’t have been. Every single thing in that dream felt too real to not be something. If it wasn’t real events it was at least a prophecy. Maybe it was God.

It had to be.

I haven’t been to church in a long time. I lost my way with the man upstairs. Religion’s never been my forte. You’d think a catholic school from five to fifteen would make a difference but it didn’t at all. My Mother had them dreams all the time though. I’d constantly hear “Vic, I had this vision.” Sometimes, they’d be true, sometimes they’d be false. Now, I guess it was my turn. The amount of adrenaline surging through my body was insane. My heads-up indicator was giving me insane reads on my physiology.

The phone rang. It felt like Deja-vu.

It was probably Fabien. I was expecting him to say the exact same thing he did in the dream. It made me tense. I cleared my voice, coughing out twice.

“Victor Brownsley, Precinct - “

“Vic, brother quit with that formal shit. I’ve been calling you for twenty-five minutes now.” He didn’t even let me finish speaking. Just went straight into berating me. It felt more natural, more like Fabien. The dream didn’t portray him right, he was colder, more detached. Even before... before he killed me. Fabien’s always been a lively guy. Despite all of this, he still isn’t jaded.

“It’s lunch man. Can you please get out of the Precinct? You owe me that sandwich from Gracie Lu’s. You ain’t getting off easy at all, brother.” He chuckles down the phone.

I’ll admit. I did too.

What happened last week was that while we were on our break, some shooting took place. I was behind the wheel and I sped off straight after them. I drove so recklessly that Fabe’s sandwich flew straight out his hands all over the windscreen, he lost the sandwich and I lost the suspects. We found it hilarious, our boss Sanders, he hated it. He said we were terrible examples, the longest tenured being the most incompetent he’d ever had the “displeasure of being in charge of.”

That is one thing though. The dream felt like it was a month long. I’d been sure of the fact there was no crime for at least a month while I was out. I even forgot that story. That’s just how dreams are, I guess. It’s only senseless in waking reality. Either way, I put my jacket on and head towards the elevator. After all that I experienced in that little dream, buying some Gracie Lu wasn’t bad at all.

“Took your time there didn’t you.” Fabien says as I get in the car. I can’t even look at him the same. It’s just awkward. I’m looking at a perfectly innocent man who also just murdered me in my mind.

“I was tired. You know how Nina is. Keeping me up with her bullshit.” He scoffs at it as I say it but honestly there’s nothing I want more than Nina’s bullshit now. If I’m alive and Fabien hasn’t shot me? Nina’s okay. You know what? I thank God, I really do. I just want to get this sandwich debt paid off and go home and see her. She’s probably still pissed off with me, I’m alright with that. She could hate the hell out of me right now.

That’s better than her having no life to hate me with.

We’re driving to Gracie Lu’s and I can’t pretend I don’t still feel this ache. I’m tense, alert. My adrenaline levels are still high as the stars. I’m looking at Fabien; not as my friend but as a threat. After that dream I just can’t. I’m all messed up and I know it. The worst part?

Only I know it.

We’re about ten minutes away when the radio picks up. It always picks up. The only thing consistent in the dream and reality? Crime is exponential in this place. I have every right to think it’s Godless. Fabien’s about to turn it off, he just wants his sandwich, to be honest, so do I. But I can’t shake off this looming danger I feel, so I tell him not to turn it off. I want to hear what the call is.

“Any available units please respond. An incident in District 6PX has occurred. Reports of gunshots from several civilians.”

My stomach dropped. I feel sick. Is this some kind of loop I’m in? I’ve watched my son play this one game. You die over and over again, sure that’s any game, but this one you have to solve something to end the loop. Maybe this is where I am now. Some kind of purgatory state. I could be wrong, maybe this could be hell. Whatever it is, I’m not prepared for it. The image of Nina dying keeps flashing back to me, my chest tightens again and again. My lips seal up.

“En-route. This is Unit BV33A and BV33E from P0AV. “ Fabien responds.

He looks over to me. Alarm bells are ringing in my head right now. The dream was some kind of gang hit, a gunfight sure, the radio didn’t say more so we’re in the dark. I couldn’t read the situation at all. I knew one thing, though.

We haven’t arrived at the crime scene yet, but I’m already suspecting him of something.

We arrived and there were several police officers surrounding a few dead bodies. Oh God I felt so quaint. I step out of the car and my leg just buckles. I fell straight down. I should have taken my time but I was just so overwhelmed. My imagination has gone rampant, coming up with new nightmares I might experience. Fabien immediately just looks at me, puzzled.

“You good Vic?”

“Yeah. Biotech just malfunctioned. I’ll get myself up, just get over there.” Bullshit I say to buy myself time. I need a breather desperately. I’m scared who it could be. Who knows? Maybe it’s Nina again. Maybe It’s Anna -

No, I can’t be thinking like this. It’s better I just face it, right?

I walk over there. I’m trembling trying to keep it together. The small flock of policemen obscure the bodies. I can’t see who it is who’s dead. I can’t see how they’ve died either. I finally get there, I push past two police officers, two more just make way. It’s just Fabien standing there, looking down. His face is perplexed, he’s calculating something. Either his next move, or how to solve the case. At last, I stood adjacent to him. I take the deepest breath possible and look down.

Relief.

It wasn’t my wife. It wasn’t any of my kids. It wasn’t anyone I know. The tension subsides a little, it’s still there, but it’s not as bad as walking over here. There’s four dead bodies, one of them laying on a wall, gun still in his loosened grip. His hand holding his stomach as he sits in a pool of blood. He died bleeding out. The other three?
