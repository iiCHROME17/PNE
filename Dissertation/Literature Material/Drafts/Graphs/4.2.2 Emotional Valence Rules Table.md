# Table 4.2.2 — Emotional Valence Computation Rules

**Source:** `Models/pne/cognitive.py` — `CognitiveLayer._calculate_emotional_valence()`

The valence starts at `0.0` and is adjusted by whichever rules fire given the NPC's personality and the player's tone scores. Rules are additive; multiple may fire in the same turn. Result is clamped to [−1.0, 1.0].

| NPC Condition | Fires when… | Tone Signal | Valence Effect | Formula | Psychological Basis |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|----------------|---------|---------------------|
| Low self-esteem | `self_esteem < 0.4` | Authority | Negative | `− authority_tone × 0.3` | Low self-esteem associates commanding tones with personal threat (Ellis, 1962) |
| Low self-esteem | `self_esteem < 0.4` | Manipulation | Negative | `− manipulation_tone × 0.5` | Susceptibility to manipulative framing; perceived exposure (Ellis, 1962) |
| External locus | `locus_of_control < 0.5` | Authority | Negative | `− authority_tone × 0.4` | External locus reads authority as situational coercion rather than legitimate direction (Heider, 1958) |
| High flexibility | `cog_flexibility > 0.6` | Diplomacy | Positive | `+ diplomacy_tone × 0.4` | Flexible thinkers respond well to reasoned, non-threatening framing (Guimond, 2006) |
| High flexibility | `cog_flexibility > 0.6` | Empathy | Positive | `+ empathy_tone × 0.3` | High flexibility correlates with empathic receptivity (Davis, 1983) |
| Rigid thinking | `cog_flexibility < 0.4` | Diplomacy | Negative | `− diplomacy_tone × 0.2` | Cognitively rigid NPCs resist persuasive framing; diplomacy reads as manipulation (Ellis, 1962) |

**Example — `open_authority` against Troy** (`self_esteem: 0.2`, `locus_of_control: 0.85`, `cog_flexibility: 0.1`):
- Rule 1 fires: `− 1.0 × 0.3 = −0.30`
- Rule 3 does not fire (locus 0.85 ≥ 0.5)
- Rule 6 fires: `− 0.0 × 0.2 = 0.00` (diplomacy tone is 0 on authority challenge)
- **Final valence: −0.30** (after clamp)

*(Actual logged value: −0.45, reflecting that the authority tone also carries a manipulation component in the `open_authority` choice's tone decomposition.)*
