# Chapter 2: Social, Legal, and Ethical Considerations

## 2.1 Overview

The Psychological Narrative Engine operates at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cognitive modelling, and interactive entertainment. This chapter addresses the social, legal, and ethical dimensions of the PNE, identifies the risks associated with its use, and outlines the mitigations built into the system's design. On balance, the PNE's social impact is assessed as net positive — particularly for smaller developers for whom deep NPC narrative has historically been inaccessible — while acknowledging genuine shifts in how narrative design labour is distributed.

---

## 2.2 Social Considerations

### 2.2.1 Impact on Smaller Studios and Independent Developers

The primary social benefit of the PNE is democratisation of narrative depth. Authoring psychologically rich NPC dialogue at scale requires dedicated writing teams and production resources that smaller studios cannot sustain. The PNE disrupts this disparity by shifting what must be authored: a developer defines NPC personality configurations and scenario graphs without writing individual dialogue lines, and the system generates adaptive NPC behaviour from that structure. For games with social simulation at their core — the class exemplified by *Stardew Valley* (ConcernedApe, 2016) — this represents a meaningful capability expansion.

### 2.2.2 Impact on Narrative Designers and Industry Labour

The impact of AI-assisted dialogue generation on narrative designers depends substantially on studio scale. For smaller studios that currently cannot employ narrative designers, the PNE fills a gap rather than displacing anyone. For mid-scale studios, the likely effect is a shift in workload: narrative designers move from writing individual dialogue lines to authoring personality models and scenario graphs — a more systems-oriented form of narrative design. For large studios such as Bethesda and Rockstar, whose productions depend on authored story arcs and scripted set-pieces, the PNE offers limited utility; their narrative designers are not at risk from a system whose target use case is opposite to their working model.

## 2.3 Legal Considerations

### 2.3.1 Software Licensing and Open Source Compliance

The PNE is built on FastAPI (MIT licence) and the Ollama runtime (MIT licence). The prototype uses Qwen2.5:3b (Apache 2.0), which permits use, modification, and commercial distribution with attribution — making it a legally straightforward choice for both research and commercial integration. No proprietary third-party APIs are called at runtime; no logs containing player data are generated; no external network requests are made during normal operation. Developers substituting alternative Ollama-compatible models carry responsibility for verifying licence terms independently.

### 2.3.2 Intellectual Property and Data Governance

The PNE does not reproduce copyrighted text or dialogue. NPC dialogue is generated at runtime from LLM inference constrained by developer-authored configurations. The thought-pattern template library is original work. The use of *Disco Elysium* as a design reference for the skill-check mechanic is analytical and transformative — the mechanic is independently implemented. No player data is collected, stored, or transmitted. Session state is held in memory for the duration of a conversation and discarded thereafter. No ethical approval for data collection was required or sought.

## 2.4 Ethical Considerations

### 2.4.1 Representation of Psychological Constructs

The PNE applies constructs drawn from cognitive psychology — belief, desire, intention, emotional state — as computational abstractions for NPC modelling. These constructs are used symbolically and are not intended to constitute clinical representations of human psychology. NPC personality parameters are design tools, not psychological assessments. Developers integrating the system carry responsibility for ensuring that NPC characterisation does not reproduce harmful stereotypes or present psychologically distressing content without appropriate content warnings.

### 2.4.2 Transparency of AI-Generated Content

Dialogue generated by the PNE is produced by a large language model at runtime, constrained by developer configurations but not authored line-by-line. Whether players should be informed that NPC dialogue is AI-generated is a live question in the games industry. This project takes no prescriptive position on disclosure, which is properly a matter for the integrating developer and applicable consumer protection regulation. Transparency about AI-generated content is, however, consistent with emerging best practice and is recommended.

## 2.5 Risk Management

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| LLM generates off-character or incoherent dialogue | Medium | Medium | Closed intention vocabulary constrains generation; system prompt encodes NPC BDI state |
| Model hallucination breaks narrative coherence | Medium | High | FSM routing is independent of LLM output; judgement score is computed from BDI outcomes |
| Ollama model licence non-compliance | Low | High | Licence terms documented per model; developers advised to verify prior to deployment |
| LLM inference latency degrades player experience | Medium | Medium | WebSocket streaming delivers tokens progressively; offline mode available |
| NPC characterisation reproduces harmful stereotypes | Low | High | Personality parameters are abstract configurations; content responsibility rests with scenario authors |

---

## 2.6 Summary

The social, legal, and ethical profile of the PNE is largely benign. Its primary social effect is extending narrative design capability to developers who currently lack it. Legal compliance is straightforward given the open-source stack and absence of data collection. The principal ethical obligations — responsible characterisation and transparency about AI-generated content — rest with integrating developers. The most significant technical risk, LLM incoherence, is structurally mitigated by the architecture's separation of reasoning from generation.
